# Reality-is-a-simulation-Quantum-analysis
Exploring time, events and external quantum influences in a simulated universe

A conceptual–experimental project exploring the hypothesis that reality may behave like a simulation.  
Using quantum principles, computational models and unconventional experiments, this repository investigates  
whether the structure of the universe shows signatures of discretization, information limits or “rendering on observation”.  
Not a claim — a framework for analysis.  

This project begins with a simple yet radical question:  
if reality were a simulation, what traces would it leave in the quantum structure of time?

This is not about religion, metaphysics or science fiction narratives.  
It is a scientific–speculative investigation that combines quantum models, numerical simulations  
and information theory to explore how a simulated universe might behave.

The working hypothesis is that our universe can be viewed as an internal quantum system,  
embedded in an external level — a “Player One”, or more simply a higher environment —  
that sets conditions, observes, records and possibly recalculates parts of the timeline.

If information flows from the internal system to the external one (observation, measurement, global states),  
could there also be a return flow?  
Is it possible that past or future events are not entirely fixed, but depend on quantum conditions  
that do not belong solely to our level?

This repository combines three research directions:

- **Quantum Listening Time**  
  Time as a process of “listening”: reality defines itself when it is interrogated.

- **Quantum Time Dilation & Reshaping**  
  Time as a dynamic structure that can stretch, compress or reorganize  
  according to informational constraints or external conditions.

- **Quantum Entanglement Across Events**  
  Non‑local correlations not only in space, but also between events in different times,  
  as if the timeline were a global object rather than a rigid sequence.

The goal is not to prove that we live in a simulation.  
The goal is to build models, conceptual experiments and simulations that show  
how a universe would behave *if* it were simulated —  
and what signatures, anomalies or patterns might emerge.

A speculative project, yes.  


But grounded in physics, information theory and the curiosity to see  
whether the “chain” between the internal observer and the external level  
can at least be glimpsed.
